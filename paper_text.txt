      Machine Learning­Based Image Copyright Detection System

                           Student Name: ChengYuan ZhuClass: 212242
                           Supervisor: XiaoXing Wan

Abstract: At present, with the rapid development of Internet technology, image data across digital

platforms has shown an explosive growth trend. Brand logos are a crucial part of a company's visual
image and also a key component of its intellectual property rights. Nowadays, they can often be
seen in digital content, which has made trademark infringement increasingly common. The
traditional methods relying on manual verification and expert appraisal have encountered
challenges in terms of efficiency and objectivity when dealing with large-scale image analysis and
complex label changes, and are unable to meet the requirements of modern copyright protection. To
solve this problem, a machine learning-driven image copyright detection system, This system
mainly focuses on brand identity recognition and similarity analysis, and relies on computational
models to improve the accuracy of detection and the degree of automation.

      The system uses ResNet50 as the core feature extraction network. By normalizing the feature
vectors, the stability and reliability of similarity calculation are guaranteed. During the process of
similarity evaluation, cosine similarity measurement can accurately quantify the visual matching
situation between the query image and the database entries. To solve the problem of multi-variant
logo recognition, a "brand maximization matching" strategy is adopted. This strategy can
effectively identify different logo styles under the same brand.

      In addition to the basic functions such as image upload and comparison, this system also
integrates the logo feature database management and the visualization result display module
together. This enhances the flexibility during operation. When conducting visualization processing,
the difference magnification strategy combines nonlinear transformation and comparison similarity
graphs for use. This approach highlights the subtle differences existing between highly similar
matches, which is to provide support for decision-making.

      After evaluating the public identification dataset in this paper, it is found that this system
performs well in terms of recognition accuracy, processing speed, and user experience. This
solution is both technologically innovative and practical, providing an efficient and scalable
intelligent tool for trademark protection.

Keywords: Image Copyright Detection; Logo Recognition; Machine Learning; ResNet50
